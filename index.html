Her Story

Winson Hudson was a courageous civil rights activist from Harmony, Mississippi, who dedicated over 50 years of her life to fighting for voting rights, education, and racial justice during the Jim Crow era and beyond.

Born into a sharecropping family, Winson overcame poverty and segregation to become a key figure in the Mississippi Freedom Movement. Alongside her husband, James "Doc" Hudson, she registered Black voters, organized community meetings, and stood against the Ku Klux Klan despite facing constant threats and intimidation.

Her memoir, "Mississippi Harmony: Memoirs of a Freedom Fighter," co-written with Constance Curry, documents her extraordinary journey and the struggles of African Americans in the Deep South. Winson's unwavering commitment to justice and equality continues to inspire generations of activists.

Biography

Winson Hudson (1916-2004) was born into a sharecropping family in Harmony, Mississippi, where she experienced firsthand the brutal realities of Jim Crow segregation. Despite these circumstances, she developed an unwavering commitment to justice that would define her entire life.

In the 1960s, Winson became a pillar of the Mississippi Freedom Movement. She registered Black voters despite facing constant intimidation, economic retaliation, and threats of violence from the Ku Klux Klan. Her home became a safe haven for civil rights workers during the Freedom Summer of 1964, and she was instrumental in organizing freedom schools.

Beyond voting rights, Winson fought tirelessly for school desegregation and quality education for Black children. She understood that lasting change required transforming institutions and empowering people through education.

Her 2002 memoir, "Mississippi Harmony: Memoirs of a Freedom Fighter," co-written with Constance Curry, stands as a powerful testament to her life and the broader struggle for civil rights. Through her writing, she preserved the stories of countless unsung heroes and documented the courage of ordinary people who achieved extraordinary change.
